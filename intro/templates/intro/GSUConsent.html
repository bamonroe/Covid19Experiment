Title: Beliefs Over Population Health and Outcomes

Principal Investigator: Professor Glenn W. Harrison, Department of Risk Management & Insurance and Center for the Economic Analysis of Risk, Georgia State University

Co-Investigators:

  • Dr. Brian Monroe, Fellow of the Center for the Economic Analysis of Risk, Georgia State University
  • Dr. Mark Schneider, Center for the Economic Analysis of Risk, Georgia State University
  • Dr. J. Todd Swarthout, Department of Economics and Experimental Economics Center, Georgia State University

Sponsor: Center for the Economic Analysis of Risk, Georgia State University

Purpose
You are invited to participate in a research study. The purpose of the study is to better understand the risk perception and beliefs of individuals over population health risks and health outcomes. You are invited to take part in this research study because you have replied to our recruiting e-mail and were sent back an invitation with a customized web-link to participate today. A total of up to 1,000 people will take part in this study.

Procedures
Participation in this study should require no more than three hours of your time today to complete. You may do this all in one sitting or at different intervals over the 24-hour period communicated in your invitation email. However you choose, the study must be done within this time frame to be considered complete for payment.

If you decide to take part, you will participate in 2 questionnaires and 4 incentivized tasks associated with this study. The questionnaires include a demographic and questions related to health risks and health outcomes. For the incentivized tasks in todayâ€™s session, you will make a series of choices. One incentivized task will ask you about your beliefs regarding health risks and health outcomes. The other incentivized tasks involve choosing between prospects with varying monetary prizes and chances of winning each prize. Prizes are either dated today or at some future date. You will be given instructions that describe in greater detail each task ahead of the decisions you will make.

This is a web-based study that you have been invited to participate in today through an invitation that contained a customized web-link. You will need that web-link, internet access and a connected device with an internet browser to participate. The customized web-link will be active for 24 hours from the study session start time documented in your invitation email. Within that 24-hour period, you can access the study and pick back up where you left off by clicking on your customized web-link. After that period the link will deactivate, and you can no longer actively participate in the study. If you complete the study, you may revisit the link to view a receipt sheet for the incentivized task decisions selected for payment. That screen contains no personally identifiable information.

Future Research
Researchers will remove information that may identify you and may use your data for future research. If we do this, we will not ask for any additional consent for you.

Risks
In this study, you will not have any more risks than you would in a normal day of life.

Benefits
Participation in this study may not benefit you personally. Overall, we expect that this research will benefit society by advancing scientific knowledge in the areas of risk perception and elicitation of individual's beliefs over population health risks and health outcomes.

Alternatives
The alternative to taking part in this study is to not take part in the study.

Compensation and Payment
You will receive a fixed participation payment for completing this study today. The amount of the fixed payment was communicated to you in the invitation email along with your customized web-link that you used to access this study today. In addition to the participation payment for completing the study, there are 4 incentivized tasks. Each incentivized task has a potential to get you additional earnings either today or in the future. The amount of additional earnings depends on your responses, chance, and actualized outcomes. To qualify for any additional earnings, you must complete the study in its entirety.

Payments from this study will be made at the appropriate date and sent to you via Venmo or PayPal. Upon entry into the study you will choose a primary and an (optional) secondary method from those options to receive any amounts owed. If there is trouble with the first method, we will try the backup. This information is for payment purposes and held separate from your response data. After completing the study, any payments owed and marked payable as of today will be verified by authorized research staff and sent within 24 hours. Task payments marked payable on a future date will be verified by authorized research staff and sent on that future date. If a task payment is dependent on the outcome of a future event, and we only know the true outcome after a specified date in the future, research staff will verify the true outcome and make payment within 14 days of that date passing. Upon completion of the study you may revisit the custom web-link sent in your invitation to view a receipt sheet for the incentivized task decisions selected for payment and the dates associated with them.

The research team provides PayPal and Venmo as options so that you can be paid quickly and conveniently. Both are private companies and Georgia State University has no authority to handle any disputes you may have with them, nor take any responsibility for your interactions with them.

Voluntary Participation and Withdrawal
Participation in this study is voluntary. You do not have to be in this study. If you decide to be in the study and change your mind, you have the right to stop participating at any time. To qualify for the fixed participation payment and any additional earnings, you must complete the study in its entirety.

Confidentiality
We will keep your records private to the extent allowed by law. The PIs and members of the study team will have access to the information you provide. Additionally, the GSU Institutional Review Board, the Office for Human Research Protection (OHRP), and Faculty and Staff of the Center for the Economic Analysis of Risk may have access to the information you provide.

We will use a code assigned to you rather than your name on study records. We will keep a separate list that links your name to this code. We will use this list to link your responses today to the responses you provided in the previous session in this study. We will keep this list secure and stored in a separate location than the response data. Only the PIs and members of this study team will have access to this list. This list will be destroyed after experiment sessions are completed and subject payments have been submitted for reimbursement.

When we present or publish the results of this study, we will not use your name or other information that may identify you. Your name and other facts that might point to you will not appear when we present this study or publish its results. The findings will be summarized and reported in group form. You will not be identified personally.

Contact Information
Contact Mark Schneider at 404-413-7463 or cear@gsu.edu if you have questions about the study or your part in it, or if you have questions, concerns, or complaints about the study or if you think you have been harmed by the study. Contact the GSU Office of Human Research Protections at 404-413-3500 or irb@gsu.edu if you have questions about your rights as a research participant, or if you have questions, concerns, or complaints about the research.
